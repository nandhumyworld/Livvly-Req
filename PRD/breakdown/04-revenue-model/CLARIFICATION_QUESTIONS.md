## Revenue Streams (5 questions)

### Q1: Multi-Pack Bundle Offers
**Question:** Should LIVVLY offer bundle discounts like "Buy 5x ₹99 packages, get 20% off total"?

**Options:**
- A) Yes - Multi-pack encourages bulk purchasing from power users
- B) No - Keep pricing simple; avoid complexity
- C) Limited - Only for seasonal promotions, not standard offering

**Implications:** ARPU impact, inventory management, churn analysis

---

### Q2: Regional Pricing Variation
**Question:** Should coin prices vary by region (cheaper in Tier-3 cities)?

**Current:** Uniform ₹99, ₹299, ₹499, ₹999 everywhere

**Options:**
- A) Uniform pricing (simplicity, brand consistency)
- B) Regional variation (cheaper in Tier-3 for higher conversion)
- C) Seasonal variation (cheaper during off-peak)

**Implications:** CAC, ARPU, market perception

---

### Q3: Ad Network Strategy
**Question:** Direct integration with individual ad networks (Google AdMob, Facebook Audience Network) or use aggregator (AdPel, Google Mobile Ads)?

**Options:**
- A) Direct with Google AdMob (90%+ of volume)
- B) Multiple networks (diversification, risk management)
- C) Aggregator (less operational complexity)

**Implications:** Revenue per ad, fill rates, operational burden

---

### Q4: Crypto/Blockchain Rewards
**Question:** Should LIVVLY offer blockchain-based rewards or NFT tips?

**Options:**
- A) No - Blockchain too complex for India market
- B) Future phase - Consider in Year 2+
- C) Yes - Early differentiator, appeals to tech-savvy users

**Implications:** Regulatory risk, user adoption, technical complexity

---

### Q5: Seasonal Promotions
**Question:** Should revenue model support seasonal coin sales (50% off during Diwali, Pongal)?

**Options:**
- A) Yes - Align with festivals for revenue spikes
- B) No - Regular pricing; stability over peaks
- C) Limited - Only for user acquisition, not monetization

**Implications:** Revenue volatility, creator expectations, user behavior

## Unit Economics (4 questions)

### Q6: Geographic CAC Variance
**Question:** Should CAC targets vary by region?

**Current assumption:** Blended ₹20-50 nationwide

**Options:**
- A) Uniform CAC target
- B) Regional buckets (Tier-1: ₹50, Tier-2: ₹40, Tier-3: ₹30)
- C) Data-driven (actual CAC by region, then optimize)

**Implications:** Marketing spend allocation, geographic prioritization

---

### Q7: Seasonal Cohort Retention
**Question:** How do we handle seasonal user cohorts (e.g., college users sign up in Jan)?

**Context:** College students might have different retention profiles than working professionals

**Options:**
- A) Single LTV model for all users
- B) Segment-specific LTV (college vs. working vs. elderly)
- C) Cohort-based LTV reassessment quarterly

**Implications:** Forecasting accuracy, retention strategy

---

### Q8: Creator vs. User ARPU
**Question:** Should creators have separate ARPU tracking?

**Context:** Creators earn from gifting, not coin purchase. Different economics apply.

**Options:**
- A) Single ARPU (aggregate all revenue)
- B) Separate ARPU for users and creators (split analysis)
- C) Three-part: Regular users, VIP/Premium subscribers, Creators

**Implications:** Forecasting clarity, segment optimization

---

### Q9: Viral Coefficient Target
**Question:** What's LIVVLY's viral coefficient target? (>1.0 = exponential growth)

**Context:** Current model assumes <1.0 (paid + organic); could optimize for virality

**Options:**
- A) <1.0 - Sustainable but managed growth
- B) 0.8-1.2 range - Aim for moderate virality
- C) >1.0 - Go viral, exponential adoption

**Implications:** Product design (sharing mechanics), CAC efficiency, growth rate
